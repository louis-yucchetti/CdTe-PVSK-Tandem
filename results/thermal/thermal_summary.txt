Thermal PVSK/CdTe analysis summary
=================================

Available simulation range: 300.00-350.00 K
Baseline reported here: 300.00 K (26.85 C)
Target reported here: 338.15 K (65.00 C)
The sweep starts at 300 K, so exact STC 25.00 C = 298.15 K is not present in the dataset.

Sub-cell thermal coefficients:
- PVSK top Voc slope = -1.373 mV/K (-0.107 %/K)
- CdTe filtered Voc slope = -2.761 mV/K (-0.349 %/K)
- 2T tandem Voc slope = -4.134 mV/K

Power thermal coefficients (gamma for Pmax):
- PVSK top gamma_Pmax = -0.02990 mW/cm2/K (-0.138 %/K)
- CdTe filtered gamma_Pmax = -0.01377 mW/cm2/K (-0.433 %/K)
- 2T tandem gamma_Pmax = -0.02079 mW/cm2/K (-0.222 %/K)
- 4T equivalent gamma_Pmax = -0.04367 mW/cm2/K (-0.176 %/K)
- Because Pin is fixed at 100 mW/cm2 in this workflow, eta and Pmax are numerically identical, but gamma_Pmax is now reported explicitly to avoid ambiguity.

Why Voc drops faster in the CdTe bottom cell:
- At 300 K, Voc is 1.2854 V for PVSK but only 0.7915 V for filtered CdTe.
- The non-bulk share of total recombination at Voc is 0.71% for PVSK at 300 K and 0.74% at 65.00 C.
- The same quantity is 6.24% for CdTe at 300 K and 7.13% at 65.00 C.
- Both cells are SRH-dominated in SCAPS, but the CdTe bottom cell carries a much larger thermally growing minority-contact leakage term.
- The guessed PVSK absorber from the local device file is '(Cs-FA-MA)(4-81-15)Pb(I-Br)_3(83-17)' with nominal Eg ~ 1.600 eV.
- The guessed CdTe absorber from the local device file is 'CdTe' with nominal Eg ~ 1.500 eV.
- The lower-gap CdTe absorber also has a smaller Voc headroom, so the same thermally activated dark-current rise costs it more voltage.

Current mismatch drift:
- Jtop - Jbottom = 15.1400 mA/cm2 at 300 K and 15.1692 mA/cm2 at 65.00 C. The gap widens by 0.0293 mA/cm2 (0.193%).
- Jtop / Jbottom = 3.8021 at 300 K and 3.8195 at 65.00 C. The ratio widens by 0.0174 (0.457%).
- Jrec at 0 V changes from 3.9774 to 3.9717 mA/cm2 in PVSK, but from 0.2742 to 0.2971 mA/cm2 in CdTe.
- That is why the drift widens rather than closes: the top-cell current stays nearly flat, while the filtered bottom-cell current slowly erodes with temperature.

Tandem performance at the target temperature:
- 2T efficiency changes from 9.378% to 8.592%.
- 4T equivalent efficiency changes from 24.776% to 23.113%.
